# Academic References

This document provides a comprehensive bibliography of academic papers, books, and resources that inform the theoretical foundations and implementation of this forecasting framework.

## Proper Scoring Rules

1. **Gneiting, T., & Raftery, A. E. (2007)**. Strictly proper scoring rules, prediction, and estimation. *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, 102(477), 359-378.
   - Foundation for proper scoring rules in probabilistic forecasting
   - Theoretical justification for Brier Score, Log Score, CRPS

2. **Brier, G. W. (1950)**. Verification of forecasts expressed in terms of probability. *Monthly Weather Review*, 78(1), 1-3.
   - Original formulation of the Brier Score

3. **Hersbach, H. (2000)**. Decomposition of the continuous ranked probability score for ensemble prediction systems. *Weather and Forecasting*, 15(5), 559-570.
   - CRPS decomposition and interpretation

## Forecast Combination

4. **Timmermann, A. (2006)**. Forecast combinations. *Handbook of Economic Forecasting*, 1, 135-196.
   - Comprehensive review of forecast combination methods
   - Weighted averaging, regression-based combination

5. **Bates, J. M., & Granger, C. W. J. (1969)**. The combination of forecasts. *Journal of the Operational Research Society*, 20(4), 451-468.
   - Foundational work on forecast combination

6. **Stock, J. H., & Watson, M. W. (2004)**. Combination forecasts of output growth in a seven-country data set. *Journal of Forecasting*, 23(6), 405-430.
   - Empirical evidence on forecast combination benefits

## Calibration

7. **Platt, J. (1999)**. Probabilistic outputs for support vector machines and comparisons to regularized likelihood methods. *Advances in Large Margin Classifiers*, 10(3), 61-74.
   - Platt scaling for probability calibration

8. **Zadrozny, B., & Elkan, C. (2002)**. Transforming classifier scores into accurate multiclass probability estimates. *Proceedings of the 8th ACM SIGKDD International Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining*, 694-699.
   - Isotonic regression for calibration

9. **Guo, C., et al. (2017)**. On calibration of modern neural networks. *International Conference on Machine Learning*, 1321-1330.
   - Temperature scaling and modern calibration techniques

## Statistical Tests

16. **Diebold, F. X., & Mariano, R. S. (1995)**. Comparing predictive accuracy. *Journal of Business & Economic Statistics*, 13(3), 253-263.
    - Diebold-Mariano test for forecast comparison

17. **Ljung, G. M., & Box, G. E. P. (1978)**. On a measure of lack of fit in time series models. *Biometrika*, 65(2), 297-303.
    - Ljung-Box test for autocorrelation

18. **Dickey, D. A., & Fuller, W. A. (1979)**. Distribution of the estimators for autoregressive time series with a unit root. *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, 74(366a), 427-431.
    - Augmented Dickey-Fuller test
